**Prior decisions affecting this plan:**

- Keep the stack on Next.js App Router + Neon + Drizzle; do not introduce Clerk, Supabase Auth, Prisma, or a separate auth datastore.
- Keep the shell server-first with only small client islands, so auth/session reads and authorization stay server-side by default.
- The current schema is split by domain module and already has business and membership tables; extend those modules instead of collapsing identity into one catch-all file.
- Use integer-cents money, enum lifecycle state, and explicit relational tables consistently; Phase 2 should keep that modeling discipline for auth and onboarding state.

**Deferred issues being addressed:**

- Research and plan Phase 2 auth and onboarding.

**Concerns being verified:**

- The auth stack must fit the live Vercel + Neon deployment path established in Phase 1 without inventing a second hosting model.
</context>
